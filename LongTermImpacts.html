Long Term Impacts


The immediate effects that came as a result of this new frontier were greater opportunities for better education. Prior to the invention of the Web, researchers had the ability to share information from only one computer to the other. After the Web was created, anyone could send anything, to anyone, at any time. This led to endless possibilities for research that were never there before. In fact, if a researcher had completed a study, now another researcher could find the same study. Prior to the creation of the internet, stumbling upon a study would simply just not be possible. In short, the creation of the internet led to data being spread faster than ever.

​​​​​​​In addition, with a new environment in the internet, competition has gone through the roof. Whether it be between one browser and another, or different internet providers, the Web has created a whole new ecosystem. For example, Amazon, an E-commerce company from America, is less than 30 years old. Despite this, the company has a value of over one trillion dollars!​​​​​​​

"96 percent of Americans have purchased something online in their lives” (George Washington University).

“No product is made today, no person moves today, no person moves today, nothing is collected, analyzed or communicated without some ‘digital technology’ being an integral part of it. That, in itself, speaks to the overwhelming ‘value’ of digital technology.” (Louis Rossetto, Former Editor in Chief of Wired Magazine).

It should also be mentioned that without the creation of the World Wide Web, social media would not be as big as it is. The impact of the two on modern day society is profound. Social media, whether it be a fifteen or fifty year old, is universally used and appreciated in Western society. Companies such as Google and Facebook are common household names purely based on how quickly they spread. Without the creation of the Web, it would be a very different tale. 

Lastly, the Web still has a bright future ahead of it. Technologies such as the Metaverse are changing the way that we think about the internet to the very day. It essentially is a three dimensional, virtual reality experience that lets the user play games or shop online. There is a headset that Facebook’s parent company, Meta, sells. Hence, a potential future term impact of the creation of the World Wide Web is the Metaverse.

"The metaverse refers to virtual, 3D environments where people can meet and interact using digital personas” (Women of Color Magazine).
